Redox Titration
Potassium permanganate (KMnO₄) titrations are self-indicating — the purple MnO₄⁻ ion is reduced to colourless Mn²⁺ in acidic solution. At the endpoint, the first excess drop of KMnO₄ turns the solution permanently pink/purple. No indicator is needed.

Back Titration
A back titration is used when the analyte is insoluble, impure, or reacts too slowly for direct titration. An excess of a known reagent is added, and the unreacted excess is titrated. The amount that reacted is found by difference.
