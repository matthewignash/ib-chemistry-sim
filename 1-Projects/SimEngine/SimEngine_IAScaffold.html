Rubric Self-Check — Criterion 1: Research Design (6 marks)
Read each descriptor and select the one that best matches your current work. The IB uses a "best-fit" approach.
5-6 Marks
The research design is clear and the methodology is appropriate and justified. The research question is focused, variables are clearly identified with appropriate ranges, and the method enables collection of sufficient, relevant data.
3-4 Marks
The research design is mostly clear with a methodology that is mostly appropriate. The research question is adequate, most variables are identified, and the method enables collection of relevant data but with some gaps.
1-2 Marks
The research design is partially clear with limited methodology. The research question may be vague, variables are incomplete, or the method has significant gaps.
0 Marks
The research design is not clear or not present. The student has not demonstrated understanding of the requirements.
Marking guidance: Look for a focused, specific RQ with measurable variables. Method must be detailed enough for replication. 5-6 requires justified choices (why this range, why this equipment). Common issue: students describe what they did rather than writing a replicable method.
Raw Data Table
Record all raw data with correct units and uncertainties. Include repeat trials for each value of the independent variable.
Guidance: Raw data must include units and uncertainties in column headers. Data should be recorded to the precision of the instrument, not rounded.
Uncertainty Guidance
Key formulas:
Absolute uncertainty = instrument precision (or half smallest division)
% uncertainty = (absolute uncertainty / measured value) × 100
Addition/subtraction: add absolute uncertainties
Multiplication/division: add percentage uncertainties

Common mistakes: Forgetting to double burette uncertainty (2 readings), using wrong number of significant figures, confusing absolute and percentage uncertainty.

Rubric Self-Check — Criterion 4: Evaluation (6 marks)
5-6 Marks
The evaluation is detailed and relevant. Strengths and weaknesses are specific and well-explained. Improvements are realistic and justified. The extension is a genuine further investigation.
3-4 Marks
The evaluation is mostly relevant. Some weaknesses and improvements are identified but may lack specificity or justification.
1-2 Marks
The evaluation is superficial. Weaknesses are generic or vague. Improvements are unrealistic or unjustified.
0 Marks
No evaluation is present or it is irrelevant to the investigation.
