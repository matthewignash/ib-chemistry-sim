Rubric Self-Check — Criterion 1: Research Design (6 marks)
Read each descriptor and select the one that best matches your current work. The IB uses a "best-fit" approach.
5-6 Marks
The research design is clear and the methodology is appropriate and justified. The research question is focused, variables are clearly identified with appropriate ranges, and the method enables collection of sufficient, relevant data.
3-4 Marks
The research design is mostly clear with a methodology that is mostly appropriate. The research question is adequate, most variables are identified, and the method enables collection of relevant data but with some gaps.
1-2 Marks
The research design is partially clear with limited methodology. The research question may be vague, variables are incomplete, or the method has significant gaps.
0 Marks
The research design is not clear or not present. The student has not demonstrated understanding of the requirements.
Marking guidance: Look for a focused, specific RQ with measurable variables. Method must be detailed enough for replication. 5-6 requires justified choices (why this range, why this equipment). Common issue: students describe what they did rather than writing a replicable method.

Before recording data, identify the absolute uncertainty of each instrument you will use.

Raw Data Table
Record all raw data with correct units and uncertainties. Include repeat trials for each value of the independent variable.
Guidance: Raw data must include units and uncertainties in column headers. Data should be recorded to the precision of the instrument, not rounded.
Uncertainty Guidance
Key formulas:
Absolute uncertainty = instrument precision (or half smallest division)
% uncertainty = (absolute uncertainty / measured value) × 100
Addition/subtraction: add absolute uncertainties
Multiplication/division: add percentage uncertainties
Open Uncertainty Trainer ↗
Common mistakes: Forgetting to double burette uncertainty (2 readings), using wrong number of significant figures, confusing absolute and percentage uncertainty.
HL Extension: Standard Deviation
After calculating your mean, calculate the standard deviation of your repeat trials to assess precision.
Standard Deviation Formula
s = √[ Σ(xi − x̄)² / (n − 1) ]
Range vs Instrument Uncertainty
Compare the half-range of your trials to the instrument uncertainty. Use whichever is larger as your overall uncertainty.
HL guidance: Students should recognise that random uncertainty is better estimated from the spread of data (half-range or standard deviation) than from instrument precision alone. If the half-range exceeds instrument uncertainty, it indicates significant random error.

Rubric Self-Check — Criterion 2: Data Collection & Processing (6 marks)
Read each descriptor and select the one that best matches your current data collection and processing work.
5-6 Marks
Raw data is recorded appropriately with correct units and uncertainties. Data processing is carried out correctly and presentation is clear and appropriate (graphs with labelled axes, error bars, and best-fit lines).
3-4 Marks
Raw data is recorded but with some errors in units or uncertainties. Processing is mostly correct but presentation has gaps (missing labels, no error bars).
1-2 Marks
Data collection is limited. Processing contains significant errors or is incomplete. Presentation is unclear or inappropriate.
0 Marks
No data or processing is present, or it is irrelevant to the investigation.
Marking guidance: 5-6 requires appropriate raw data tables (units + uncertainties in headers), correct calculations with units at every step, and a well-constructed graph. Look for error bars and identification of anomalies.

HL Extension: Correlation & Linearisation
Correlation Coefficient (R²)
If you used a line of best fit, estimate the R² value and assess the strength of correlation.
Linearisation
If your relationship is non-linear, consider whether a logarithmic or reciprocal transformation would produce a linear graph.
HL guidance: R² > 0.95 is typically considered a strong correlation. Linearisation is expected when the raw data shows a clear curve. Common transformations: ln(rate) vs 1/T for Arrhenius, ln[A] vs t for first-order, 1/[A] vs t for second-order.
Rubric Self-Check — Criterion 3: Analysis & Conclusion (6 marks)
Read each descriptor and select the one that best matches your conclusion and analysis quality.
5-6 Marks
The conclusion is justified and relevant, directly answering the RQ with specific data. A detailed scientific explanation is given with correct theory. Comparison to literature values is meaningful with a calculated percentage error.
3-4 Marks
The conclusion answers the RQ but with limited data reference. Scientific explanation is mostly correct but lacks depth. Literature comparison is present but superficial.
1-2 Marks
The conclusion is incomplete or does not clearly answer the RQ. Scientific context is vague or incorrect. No meaningful literature comparison.
0 Marks
No conclusion is present, or it is irrelevant to the investigation.
Marking guidance: 5-6 requires a conclusion that quotes specific data values with units, identifies the relationship type (linear, proportional, inverse), explains using correct chemical theory, and makes a meaningful comparison to accepted values with % error interpretation.

HL Extension: Statistical Significance
For HL, consider whether your results are statistically significant. A t-test can determine if the difference between two data sets is meaningful or due to random variation.
t-test Analysis
Compare two sets of results (e.g., two different IV values, or your result vs. literature).
HL guidance: Students should identify which data sets to compare, calculate t correctly, and interpret using a t-table at 95% confidence. The conclusion should state whether the null hypothesis (no significant difference) is accepted or rejected.

5-6 Marks
The evaluation is detailed and relevant. Strengths and weaknesses are specific and well-explained. Improvements are realistic and justified. The extension is a genuine further investigation.
3-4 Marks
The evaluation is mostly relevant. Some weaknesses and improvements are identified but may lack specificity or justification.
1-2 Marks
The evaluation is superficial. Weaknesses are generic or vague. Improvements are unrealistic or unjustified.
0 Marks
No evaluation is present or it is irrelevant to the investigation.
