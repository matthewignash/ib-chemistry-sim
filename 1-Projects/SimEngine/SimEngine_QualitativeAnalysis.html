Flame Tests — Metal Ion Identification
Flame tests identify metal ions by the characteristic colour they emit when heated in a Bunsen flame. The metal ion's electrons are excited to higher energy levels and emit photons of specific wavelengths as they return to the ground state.

Example Research Question
How can flame tests and precipitation reactions be used to identify unknown ionic compounds?
Variables
IV:
Identity of unknown compound (unknowns A–F)
DV:
Observed flame colour / precipitate formation
CVs:
Concentration of reagents (0.1 mol dm⁻³), sample size, observation conditions
Data Recording Prompt
Record observations systematically: flame colour, precipitate colour with NaOH/AgNO₃, solubility in excess. Use a results table.
