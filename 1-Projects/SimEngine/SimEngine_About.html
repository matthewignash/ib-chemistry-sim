About SimEngine

A free suite of interactive chemistry simulations

SimEngine is a free, open suite of 38 interactive chemistry simulations covering the full IB Chemistry 2025 syllabus. It was built as a teaching tool first, a student reference second, and a portfolio project third.

Everything runs in the browser. No accounts, no logins, no tracking, no student data leaves the device.

Who Built This

SimEngine was built by Matthew Ignash, Head of Science at the American International School Chennai. Matthew teaches IB Chemistry and AP Chemistry and writes about AI, systems thinking, and curriculum design in international schools.

The first simulation was built in March 2026 for a lesson on electrochemistry. The full suite of 38 sims followed over the next six days, covering every subtopic of the IB Chemistry 2025 syllabus along with Lab Builder, Vernier Lab, and IA Scaffold modules for practical work and investigation design.

Core Simulations

Content sims cover every topic in the IB Chemistry 2025 syllabus: atomic structure, bonding, stoichiometry, energetics, kinetics, equilibrium, acids and bases, electrochemistry, and organic chemistry. Each sim toggles between SL and HL content and includes a teacher view with model answers.

Practical Work & Investigation

Lab Builder provides 10 guided assembly templates plus a sandbox mode for students to design their own virtual experiments. Vernier Lab simulates Go Direct sensors for data collection. IA Scaffold walks students through the full Internal Assessment investigation cycle.

Skills Trainers

Dedicated modules for graph skills, uncertainty calculations, equipment identification, and data processing. Built to be used as short warm-up activities or assessment prep.

Design Principles

  • Free to use, no accounts required, no data collection.
  • Single-file HTML so sims can be shared by link, embedded, or saved offline.
  • Accessibility baked in: ARIA labels, keyboard navigation, high-contrast visuals.
  • Teacher-first design: every sim has a built-in teacher view with model answers and common misconception notes.
  • SL and HL content toggled from a single interface so mixed-level classes can share the same link.
