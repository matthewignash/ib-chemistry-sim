Flipped Content Production: Google NotebookLM

How It Works

Upload IB guide sections, Kognity PDFs, your own notes to a NotebookLM notebook per unit. NotebookLM generates:

  • Audio Overviews — 8–12 min podcast-style summaries for pre-class prep
  • Study Guides — AI-generated summaries with key concepts
  • FAQ sections — Common student questions from source material

Production Workflow

  1. Create one NotebookLM notebook per unit (12 notebooks total)
  2. Upload: IB guide pages, Kognity PDFs, lesson notes
  3. Generate Audio Overview per subtopic with focus prompts
  4. Create 1-page guided notes template (fill-in while listening)
  5. Upload audio + notes + checkpoint quiz to Schoology

~30 min per assignment to produce. Total: ~25–30h production for 2-year library.

What Still Needs Custom Screencasts

  • Step-by-step calculations (moles, enthalpy, pH, electrode potentials)
  • Drawing Lewis structures, VSEPR shapes, mechanisms
  • Lab technique demos

~15–20 screencasts (3–5 min each). NotebookLM covers all conceptual/reading content.

Flipped Classroom Accountability System

Load-bearing strategy. With 75h of content flipped (~2h/wk) and no extra sessions or holiday work, the Schoology pre-class system must work consistently. The first 3 weeks are critical for establishing the habit.

1. Pre-Class Checkpoint Quiz (5 min)

3–5 auto-graded Schoology questions at the START of every class with assigned flipped content. Formative — tracked in gradebook.

Key: Immediate accountability. Pattern of zeros triggers a conversation, not a penalty.

2. Entry Ticket Gate

Below 60% on checkpoint → 5-min catch-up while prepared students begin practice. No full-class lecture bail-out.

Key: Natural consequence. Prepared students aren’t held back.

3. Audio + Guided Notes

Each flipped assignment: NotebookLM audio (8–12 min) + guided notes template + checkpoint quiz. Due 11pm night before class. Total: 30–45 min.

Key: Audio format is accessible. Guided notes prove engagement. Fits in one homework session.

4. Peer Groups (5 min)

Groups of 3–4 compare checkpoint results at start of class. Social accountability is more effective than teacher pressure.

Key: Peer pressure for teens > teacher pressure. Also serves as retrieval practice.

5. Required vs. Recommended

~70% of flipped content is “Required” (has checkpoint quiz). ~30% is “Recommended” (enrichment). Required = 2–3 assignments/wk within homework budget.

Key: Students take Required seriously. Keeps signal clear.

6. No Homework Cliff

No assignments over DI week, fall break, winter break, or spring break. Flipped content resumes the week before return with a “warm-up” assignment.

Key: Respects boundaries. Maintains trust in the system.

Design Decisions (v5 changes)

3 Events Before DI (Both Years)

S1/S3.2 gets a full formative→summative cycle in 4 weeks. Then 2–3 periods of the next unit before a formative as event #3. The formative-to-summative gap spans DI + fall break (~3 weeks), which is unusual but workable: students get feedback, a break to process, then a summative on return.

Mock = 2 Classes, Not 4

P1A+P1B in one period, P2 in another = 2.6h total. Saves ~1.4h vs. full-length simulation. One week of dedicated revision precedes the mock. Mock covers all content through R3.2 (everything taught before it).

R3.3+R3.4 Taught After Mock

Organic mechanisms are the final new content, taught March–April post-mock. Students have the motivation of just having seen the exam format. This also relieves pressure on the pre-mock schedule and gives more revision time. Assessment 12 is the last graded event before IB exams.

Spiral Assessment (70–80% / 20–30%)

After the first unit, every unit assessment includes 20–30% from previously taught content. This builds cumulative retention and reduces the need for dedicated review time. Assessment 1 (S1) is 100% S1 since there’s nothing prior.

Homework Budget: 2h/wk Steady State

With 3–4 nights × 30–45 min = 1.5–3h/wk capacity. Y1 weeks 1–3 runs hot (~3h/wk) to front-load S1 flipped content and establish the habit. Settles to ~1.5–2h/wk thereafter. Y2 Sem2 shifts toward past paper practice as flipped content winds down.

Y1 Sem1 Buffer (~13h)

The first semester has the largest buffer by design. This is the semester where you’re establishing the flipped classroom routine, learning the students, and may need extra practice time. The buffer absorbs slower-than-expected pacing, additional labs, or deeper S2 practice.
